PDF TAROT READER - DESIGN DECISIONS
====================================
Designer: Riley
Project: proj_hfuezl_1765954623183

CORE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY
----------------------

1. "Mystical Meets Modern"
   The challenge was balancing whimsy with professionalism. We wanted users
   to feel delighted, not embarrassed to share their readings. Think of it
   as the difference between a cheesy fortune teller booth and a beautifully
   designed tarot deck you'd find at a design museum gift shop.

2. Generous Whitespace
   The tarot cards are the star. Everything else - buttons, badges, text -
   should recede and let the reading breathe. Dense UIs feel anxious.
   Our users are here to have fun, not fill out tax forms.

3. Restraint in Animation
   Every animation has a purpose. The card flip feels magical because it's
   the ONLY elaborate animation. If everything bounced and sparkled, nothing
   would feel special. We're building an experience, not a casino.


COLOR PALETTE DECISIONS
-----------------------

Primary: Cosmic Purple (#6B46C1)
- Deep purple conveys mysticism without being dark or heavy
- Has excellent contrast with white backgrounds
- Differentiates us from the sea of blue SaaS products
- Accessible: passes WCAG AA for text on white

Why not darker purple?
- Darker purples can feel corporate or heavy
- Our lighter purple feels approachable and fun
- Still reads as "mystical" without being goth

Card Position Colors:
- Past (Sage Green): Growth, history, what was planted
- Present (Ocean Blue): Current state, clarity, now
- Future (Twilight Rose): Warning, change, what's coming
- These aren't arbitrary - they have emotional resonance with the card meanings

Gold Accents:
- Used sparingly for stars, highlights, special moments
- Creates hierarchy and draws the eye to important elements
- Classic pairing with purple (royalty, mysticism)


TYPOGRAPHY DECISIONS
--------------------

Playfair Display for Headlines:
- Serif fonts feel editorial, timeless, slightly mystical
- Beautiful contrast with the clean sans-serif body
- The italic variant could be used for card names (extra flourish)
- Not overused - only headlines and card names

Inter for Body:
- Extremely legible at all sizes
- Variable font with excellent rendering
- Feels modern and trustworthy
- Doesn't fight for attention with the decorative elements

Type Scale (based on 1.25 ratio):
- Consistent visual rhythm
- Clear hierarchy without dramatic jumps
- Body text at 16px for comfortable reading


SPACING SYSTEM
--------------

8px grid, but with named tokens:
- Developers don't have to do math
- Consistent rhythm across all components
- Easy to adjust globally if needed

Key insight: The "breathing room" around the card spread is what makes
it feel premium. Cramped layouts feel cheap.


ANIMATION PHILOSOPHY
--------------------

Card Flip (600ms, spring easing):
- This is THE moment. The reveal.
- Spring easing adds personality - it slightly overshoots and settles
- 600ms is slow enough to feel intentional, fast enough to not drag
- Staggered entrance builds anticipation

Micro-interactions (120-150ms):
- Hover states need to feel immediate
- Too slow = laggy, too fast = jarring
- Subtle scale (1.02) + shadow creates "lift" effect

Reduced Motion Support:
- All decorative animations respect prefers-reduced-motion
- Essential state changes still work, just instant
- Accessibility isn't optional


COMPONENT DECISIONS
-------------------

DropZone:
- Large, inviting target area (min 300px height)
- Dashed border = universal "drop here" signifier
- Active state with glow makes it clear when dragging
- Max width prevents it from feeling overwhelming on large screens

Tarot Cards:
- 2:3 aspect ratio matches real tarot cards
- 220px width on desktop is large enough to read, small enough for three in a row
- Position accent (colored left edge) provides instant visual identification
- Shadow gives depth without being heavy

AuraBadge:
- Pill shape (radius-full) makes it feel like a label/tag
- Gradient background varies by aura type
- Small size keeps it from competing with cards

ExportButton:
- Primary action = primary color
- Large enough touch target for mobile (44px min)
- Clear loading and success states
- "Download" icon makes action obvious


RESPONSIVE STRATEGY
-------------------

Mobile-first thinking:
- Cards stack vertically on small screens
- Typography scales down gracefully
- Touch targets stay accessible (44px minimum)
- Reduce complexity, not features

Breakpoints:
- 640px: Mobile to tablet
- 768px: Card layout switches from vertical to horizontal
- 1024px+: Full desktop experience


PDF COVER PAGE DESIGN
---------------------

Dark background (#1A202C):
- Creates dramatic contrast with the web UI
- Looks professional when printed
- Works well in document viewers

Simplified card representations:
- Vector graphics scale perfectly
- No raster images to worry about
- Consistent rendering across PDF viewers

Stamp rotation (-3deg):
- Subtle imperfection makes it feel hand-stamped
- Adds playfulness without being chaotic
- Classic "certification" aesthetic


WHAT WE EXPLICITLY AVOIDED
--------------------------

1. Excessive gradients and glows
   - One glow effect (card hover) is elegant
   - Everything glowing is overwhelming

2. Dark mode in MVP
   - Tokens are ready for it
   - Focus on nailing light mode first

3. Complex illustrations
   - Simple geometric card symbols scale better
   - Faster to render in PDF
   - More flexible across contexts

4. Busy patterns
   - Clean surfaces let content shine
   - Patterns would compete with card artwork

5. Too many fonts
   - Two fonts (display + body) is plenty
   - Third font only for technical elements


ACCESSIBILITY NOTES
-------------------

- All colors pass WCAG AA contrast
- Focus states are visible and styled
- Card reveals are announced to screen readers
- Motion can be disabled system-wide
- Touch targets are minimum 44px
- No information conveyed by color alone

- When in doubt, add more whitespace
